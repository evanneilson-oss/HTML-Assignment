📜 A&M Traditions

A collection of time-honored traditions that define the A&M experience

Aggie Ring

One of the most recognizable symbols of Texas A&M University. The Aggie Ring tradition dates back to 1926 and is worn by over 150,000 former students.

Significance: A lifelong commitment to the Aggie network and values

Marching Band

The Fightin' Texas Aggie Band is famous for its military precision and distinctive musical arrangements. They have performed at every home football game since 1907.

Significance: Pride, discipline, and school spirit

Muster

An annual tradition held on April 21st where Aggies gather worldwide to honor fallen Aggies. The ceremony includes the reading of names and the Roll Call for the Absent.

Significance: Remembrance and unity

Midnight Yell

A coordinated cheer performed at football games by the entire crowd. Students practice complex yells and cheers that are executed in perfect unison.

Significance: School spirit and community

Bonfire

One of the largest student-built bonfire traditions in the nation. Built by students for nearly a century, it represents Aggie pride and unity.

Significance: Tradition, spirit, and camaraderie

Silver Taps

A solemn tradition held to honor the passing of an Aggie. Silver Taps is played on the steps of the Memorial Student Center in tribute.

Significance: Respect and remembrance
