Silver Taps

Silver taps has been an amazing tradition at Texas A&M University, its purpose is to honor our fallen Aggies that have passed away during the school year. Its one of our oldest tradditions and its been around since 1898. Its held on the first tuesday of every month and its an sureal experience that holds the core of aggie tradditions.

Where it is?

  1. Silver taps is held at the Texas A&M Academic Plaza.
  2. It occurs during the first tuesday of the month from September to April.

What do they do there?

  1. At silver taps they honor students who pass away during the academic year.
  2. They play "Silver taps" to the north, south, and west but never the east, symbolizing that the sun will never rise again on that student.

Who founded it?

  • Silver taps doesn't really have a single founder, it is more of a tradition that has evolved over time.
  • However, in 1929 the band director, Col. Richard J. Dunn, is the one that created the specific musical arrangement still today.
